Data collection

We have collected data from IMDB which is a database of all the cinematographic records in the entire world. We have combined this database with the TMDB API which allows us to obtain information about the revenue and budget of each movie and some information about Oscars Awards to analyze that is useful to analyze the success of movies. The TMDB API could only provide us information about the revenue and budget of around ten thousand movies. For this research question, we narrowed down to only movies and we excluded tv series, tv shows, etc.

Data Available

These are some of the available features that we could collect: genre, release year, budget, runtime, directors, writers, rating, number of votes, awards, budget, revenue

What do we mean by successfull?

For our research questions we use the term "success" multiple times. The metrics we use to measure the success of a movie are the average imdb rating, to see how people enjoyed the movie, the number of votes on imdb, which gives us an idea of how many people watched that movie, and the revenue which tells is how much money the movie made.

How did the runtime, genre distribution and success of movies change from 1915 until today?

The average runtime of movies has steadily increased since 1915.

The amount of movies released has increased almost every year since 1915. There was a significant drop in 2020, probably caused by the covid pandiemic.

Drama and comedy have been the most popular genres from 1915 even until today. Romance also used to be one of the most popular genres, but has since been surpassed by Documentaries, Thriller and Horror movies.

The average budget and revenue of movies has steadily increased over the years, peaking at an average budget of 62 Million USD$ and an average revenue of 225 Million USD$ in 2017. In contrast to that, the average rating of movies has steadily decreased. We think that this is caused by there beeing more movies beeing created every year, which leads to an overall decrease in quality.

Which are the most popular and most successfull genres, and which combinations of genres are most common?

To see how the genre of movies are distributed we looked at the genre of all movies in IMDb. Further we look at individual genres to see if there are genres that gained or lost popularity over time.

By attributing above 50% of all movies, drama is by far the most popular genre. The second and third most common genres are Comedy with 27% and Romance with 12%. It should be noted that the Percantages of all genres dont sum up to 100, since movies are labeled with up to 3 genres.

As already expected from the genre distribution, the most common combinations are combinations of drama, comedy and romance with each other and other popular genres. Notible combinations are Horror with Thriller and Biography with Documentary.

Are there differences in the IMDb ratings for movies released in different years? First of all the average rating of all movies released in year declines over the years. In the box plot one can also see that the variance in the ratings declines. Do movies get worse? We can not answer this in our research, but what we can say is, that newer movies have a higher number of reviews as seen in figure 2 with a rise of the average number of reviews per year. So older movies get reviewed less than newer ones, which may also influence the rating given in those reviews.

The distribution of oscar wins and nominations among the genres looks similar to the distributions of genres, with drama and romance having the most oscar nominations and wins, and the only genres without any nominations are very specific genres like reality-tv. Note that Oscar Nominations don't include Oscars won.

Do the runtime and the budget of a movie have a measurable impact on its success?

For an overview we look at the average budget and revenue over time. Not surprisingly both values rise over the years with a drop in 2020 when the Corona pandemic hit. Further we look at the differences between different genres.

For the rating its visible that with increasing length of a movie the average rating increases. For the number of votes and the revenue we can see that the trendline peaks at around 120 minutes. The tendline is a locally weighted regression line.
